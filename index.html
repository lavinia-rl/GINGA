Abstract

We present GINGA, a comprehensive humanoid sports dataset designed to advance research in human motion analysis and synthesis. GINGA encompasses a wide range of sports activities, captured using state-of-the-art motion capture technology, providing high-fidelity data on human movement. The dataset includes detailed annotations of joint positions, velocities, and forces, enabling researchers to explore various aspects of human motion, such as biomechanics, motor control, and performance optimization. GINGA aims to facilitate the development of novel algorithms for motion prediction, generation, and analysis, ultimately contributing to advancements in fields such as sports science, rehabilitation, and robotics.
